Q. Hack Harder... what is it, exactly?
A. We're trying to strip away the pageantry that seems to go with most computer security themed conferences in favor of a smaller, more technically-oriented gathering. It's fun to have parties, dress up in costume, drink a lot, etc. but the goal of HH is to get together and enjoy a distillation of the more technical aspects of computers, conferences, and hacking things. Rather than going to a talk and having one person broadcast their ideas to an audience, we want to have one or more presenters explain a thing for 30 or 60 minutes, and then guide the group through hands-on activities to reinforce the ideas and enjoy applying them.

Q. What sort of activities would I be doing?
A. Some of the ideas we have been considering include:

  • Decoding POCSAG with SDR: ( http://www.rtl-sdr.com/rtl-sdr-tutorial-pocsag-pager-decoding/ )
  • Zigbee Wardriving
  • Airplane stuff: ADS-B, ACARS, etc.
  • Assmbly Language workshop & games
  • Fun with wearables
  • Design & build an electronic badge

Q. How many people will be there?
A. For now, we're thinking of capping it at 50 people. The current thinking is we'd all get more out of a smaller event. Ten or fifteen people who are really interested in the activity will be more fun than just filling a room.

Q. Is this an Information Security conference?
A. No. The lines can get pretty blurry sometimes. Here we're looking for folks who enjoy technology and hacking things just because they are curious about them.
